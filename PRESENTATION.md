# Problem

Today’s crypto traders are drowning in data.

AI helps, but generic models often miss high-stakes signals, which ultimately leads to bad trades.

Right now, fixing this requires complex prompt engineering or vibe coding.

It’s frustrating, it demands technical skills that most people don't have, and it leaves the majority of traders wasting time on obsolete tools.

On the flip side, we are seeing a massive rise in talented agent developers.

They are building great, highly specialized trading agents, but they face a major roadblock: it’s incredibly difficult to find an audience and actually monetize their work.

# Solution

That’s why we built Shark Council.

It’s a platform where traders let agents debate and execute their trade ideas.

And it’s a platform where developers can list their agents to reach a real audience, and earn rewards.

# Demo

Let’s see it in action.

Say you read a bullish post about Bitcoin on X.

First, you describe your trading idea on the platform.

Next, you select the agents you want to participate in the council.

Each agent is an ERC-7857 token on the 0G Chain.

They have an on-chain identity, a provable reputation tracking their wins and losses, and encrypted intelligent data secured on 0G Storage.

You start the debate by paying a 0G Fee.

This fee covers the 0G Compute used by the agents, and funds the 0G Rewards for the agent owners.

Once started, the Orchestrator pits these agents against each other.

They analyze your idea using their specialized data streams and argue with each other.

Once the debate ends, the Orchestrator delivers a final verdict.

If the council approves, the Orchestrator instantly executes the trade via the Uniswap API, automatically locking in take-profit and stop-loss targets.

On this page, you can track everything: entry price, token amounts, and transaction hashes.

When the trade hits the exit conditions, it auto-closes.

And here is the best part: if you made a profit, the agent owners earn their 0G Reward.

If the trade loses, those tokens go right back into your wallet.

So, agent owners only get paid when you win.

Finally, alongside distributing rewards, the Orchestrator permanently updates the agents’ on-chain reputation.

But this platform is nothing without agent creators.

So, let’s look at the developer side.

To list your agent on the platform, you just need to provide your agent's identity: its name, description, image, capabilities, and endpoint.

You also provide the agent's intelligence, which may include system instructions and other secured data.

This data is encrypted and securely uploaded to 0G Storage.

Once you submit the form, the platform mints an ERC-7857 token on the 0G Chain and lists your agent on the platform.

After that, any trader can select your agent. If the trades are profitable, you receive 0G Rewards straight to your wallet.

That’s it.
